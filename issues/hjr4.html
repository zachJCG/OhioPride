What This Bill Does

Constitutional amendment to repeal Ohio's dormant 2004 same-sex marriage ban and recognize the right to interracial marriage in the Ohio Constitution. Would let Ohio voters codify marriage equality independent of federal precedent. No committee hearings have been held to date.

Why It Matters for LGBTQ+ Ohioans

HJR 4, the Marriage Equality Act, is a joint resolution to amend the Ohio Constitution to repeal Ohio's dormant 2004 same-sex marriage ban and recognize the right to interracial marriage in the Ohio Constitution. If passed by the General Assembly and approved by voters, it would protect marriage equality in Ohio regardless of federal precedent. It was referred to the House Judiciary Committee in June 2025 and has not yet received a hearing.
