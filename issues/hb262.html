What This Bill Does

HB 262 would establish a state-designated "Natural Family Month" from Mother's Day to Father's Day each year. The advocacy framing defines "natural families" as consisting of "one biological born man, woman" — a family structure that by design excludes same-sex parents and nontraditional families.

Impact on LGBTQIA+ Ohioans

This is primarily a recognition/values bill rather than a direct restrictor of civil rights. The immediate impact is stigmatic and political — using state authority to affirm a narrow definition of family that excludes LGBTQIA+ families from celebratory recognition. While litigation risk is lower than other bills in this set, it signals legislative intent.

Legal & Constitutional Risks

  • Stigmatic Harm — State endorsement of a narrow family definition that excludes LGBTQIA+ families
  • Comparatively lower litigation risk unless paired with discriminatory implementation
  • Political signaling value may be more significant than direct legal impact
