Why May 22

Launching on Harvey Milk Day.

“You have to give people hope.”

— Harvey Milk, 1978

May 22 would have been Harvey Milk's ninety-sixth birthday. He was the first openly gay person elected to public office in California, a San Francisco city supervisor, and a founding figure of the modern movement for LGBTQ+ political power. He believed in coalitions, in showing up, and in giving people something to hope for. He paid for that work with his life.

Almost half a century later, his birthday is observed as Harvey Milk Day. We can think of no better day to launch the first statewide LGBTQ+ PAC in Ohio history.

What to Expect

A launch worth showing up for.

This is a celebration and a statement: Ohio's LGBTQ+ community has political infrastructure of its own, and we're putting it to work for 2026 and beyond.

Why This Moment

Hope, organized.

Ohio Pride launches at a defining moment. In 2026, every statewide executive office and a U.S. Senate seat are on the ballot at the same time. Two constitutional amendment campaigns, the Right to Marry and the Ohio Equal Rights Amendment, are gathering signatures. Anti-LGBTQ+ legislation continues to move through the General Assembly. And the state's largest LGBTQ+ advocacy organization significantly reduced its operations last year.

The community needs a PAC that endorses, fundraises, scorecards, and turns out voters with focus and discipline. That's what we're building, and we're building it in public, starting at the Statehouse on Harvey Milk Day.
