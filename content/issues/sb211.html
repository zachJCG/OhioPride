What This Bill Does

SB 211 would designate one week each year as "Love Makes a Family Week" in Ohio, celebrating the diversity of Ohio's families — including LGBTQ+ families, adoptive families, single-parent households, grandparent-led families, and other nontraditional family structures. The bill serves as a direct counterpoint to HB 262, which seeks to establish a "Natural Family Month" defined exclusively around heterosexual, biological parentage.

Why This Matters

Ohio is home to tens of thousands of children being raised by LGBTQ+ parents, grandparents, and chosen families. SB 211 sends a clear message that all of Ohio's families deserve recognition and respect — not just those that fit a narrow, exclusionary definition. While HB 262 seeks to enshrine a hierarchy of family structures, this bill affirms that love, commitment, and stability define a family.

Context

  • HB 262, the "Natural Family Month" bill, explicitly defines family as "one biological born man and one biological born woman" — erasing LGBTQ+ families
  • SB 211 takes the opposite approach, celebrating all family forms that provide children with love and stability
  • Symbolic legislation like this shapes public discourse and signals legislative priorities
  • Over 114,000 same-sex couple households exist in Ohio according to census data
