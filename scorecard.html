      How These Grades Work

      The Quick Version

      Every Ohio state lawmaker gets three small scores, each from −5 (worst) to +5 (best):

      • Votes — how they vote on bills that affect LGBTQ+ Ohioans, on the floor and in committee.
      • Bills — what bills they sponsor or co-sponsor.
      • Voice — what they say in public, in floor speeches, and in the press.

      We add the three scores together and turn that into a grade from 0 to 100. A 50 means “no record either way yet.” A 100 means a champion. A 0 means a hostile record.

      Where The Numbers Come From

      Every score is built from official Ohio General Assembly roll call records, bill sponsorship filings, and public statements. We list our sources next to each lawmaker so you can check the work yourself.

      Floor vs. Committee

      We report floor votes and committee votes separately on every lawmaker's card. A bill often lives or dies in committee long before it reaches the floor, so committee votes count.
