Scoring Methodology

How A Score Is Built

Each legislator receives three editorial subscores, each ranging from −5 (consistently anti-equality) to +5 (consistently pro-equality). The subscores are summed into a raw figure between −15 and +15, then mapped to a 0–100 composite using the formula below. We chose three subscores rather than a long list of weighted line items because every input is grounded in primary-source evidence, and a transparent additive model is easier for journalists, candidates, and members of the public to audit than a black-box weighting scheme.

Votes
−5 to +5
Aggregated record on roll call votes affecting LGBTQ+ Ohioans, covering both floor action (passage, concurrence, override) and committee reports.
Bills
−5 to +5
Primary sponsorship and co-sponsorship of bills tracked by Ohio Pride PAC, weighted more heavily for primary authorship than for adding a name to a colleague's bill.
Voice
−5 to +5
Public statements, floor speeches, committee remarks, and press coverage that explicitly support or attack LGBTQ+ Ohioans. Silence on critical bills is treated as a neutral signal, not a positive one.

The composite score is then computed as:

score = clamp(0, 100, round(50 + (Votes + Bills + Voice) × 5))

A subscore vector of 0, 0, 0 produces a baseline composite of 50. A perfect +5, +5, +5 reaches 100; a worst-case −5, −5, −5 bottoms out at 0. Mid-cycle, a legislator with no recorded action on tracked legislation will sit near 50 by design until the record gives us reason to move them.

How Roll Calls Are Weighted Inside The Votes Subscore

When the Votes subscore is constructed, not every roll call carries the same weight. The vote breakdown displayed on each legislator card uses the following multipliers to surface which actions had the largest editorial impact. Higher-stakes procedural moments count more; preliminary or amendment-only votes count less.

  • Veto-override roll call: 1.25×
  • Concurrence or final passage: 1.00×
  • Chamber-of-origin passage: 1.00×
  • Committee report: 0.75×
  • Amendment-only vote: 0.50×
  • Introduction or referral: 0.25×

Floor Versus Committee

Floor votes and committee votes are reported in separate sections on each legislator's card, and the Voting Record header tallies them independently. We track committee action because legislation often lives or dies long before it reaches a chamber floor, and members who quietly hold a harmful bill in committee, or who advance a protective bill out of committee, deserve credit (or accountability) for that posture even when no floor vote ever materializes.

Source Hierarchy

When sources conflict, the higher-authority source prevails. The full hierarchy, in descending order:

  1. Chamber journal of record (official roll call)
  2. Legislative Service Commission bill analysis
  3. Committee minutes and clerk reports
  4. Primary-source news (hearing coverage, press events)
  5. Sponsor-authored statements and press releases
  6. Advocacy or opposition statements

Grade Scale

Once the composite score is computed, the grade is applied directly from the table above.

Data Sources

  • Ohio General Assembly official roll call records (legislature.ohio.gov)
  • Legislative Service Commission bill analyses and committee reports
  • Ohio Senate and House journals of record
  • Bill sponsorship and co-sponsorship records as filed with the Clerk of each chamber
  • Public statements, press conferences, and verified news coverage
  • Equality Ohio, TransOhio, and ACLU Ohio legislative tracking for cross-verification
