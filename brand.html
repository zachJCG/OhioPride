Section 01

Brand foundation

Ohio Pride PAC is a state-level political action committee registered with the Ohio Secretary of State, dedicated to building political power for LGBTQ+ equality across every corner of Ohio.

Mission

Endorse pro-equality candidates of any party. Mobilize voters. Fight anti-LGBTQ+ legislation in the Ohio Statehouse.

Positioning

A nonpartisan PAC that complements, not duplicates, the work of Ohio's LGBTQ+ advocacy organizations. Advocacy organizations educate and lobby; Ohio Pride PAC builds on that work by putting money and political power behind candidates and causes that will act.

Tagline

Endorse.·Mobilize.·Fight for Ohio.

Section 02

Logo & wordmark

The wordmark is built from three named glyphs in Montserrat, sitting on Navy Primary. The same three-span construction is used everywhere — in the nav, in body content, and in social assets — and is generated by the shared site template so it never drifts.

Ohio Pride PAC Primary wordmark · on Navy Primary #0F2233
Ohio Pride PAC Mono-white fallback · single-color contexts only

Construction

Glyph Weight Color Notes
Ohio Montserrat 400 White at 65% opacity Sets the steady, Ohio-rooted tone.
Pride Montserrat 700 Pure white #FFFFFF Carries the visual emphasis of the mark.
PAC Montserrat 700, uppercase Light Blue #73D7EE ~18% of full size · 2px tracking · denotes the political action committee designation.
Don't

Never fill the wordmark with the Progress Pride gradient. Don't substitute different fonts. Don't hand-edit the markup in another HTML file — call OhioPride.renderWordmark() or use the .ohp-wordmark utility class instead.

Use deliberately

Use the Progress Pride gradient as thin dividers, accent stripes, donate-button fills, and card-border hovers. Use it deliberately, never decoratively. The raw Pride color chips fail WCAG AA on Navy Primary — for accent text, prefer the on-dark tokens (--brand-text-accent, --brand-text-danger, --brand-text-success, --brand-text-warning).

Section 05

Voice & tone

Confident, not loud. Action-oriented. Complementary, not competitive. Nonpartisan, pro-equality.

In one sentence

The clear, steady voice of an Ohioan who's done waiting — informed, organized, and ready to fight for neighbors at the ballot box.

Word choice

Use

  • Pro-equality candidates
  • LGBTQ+ Ohioans and their families
  • Ally / Pro-equality leader
  • Ohio Statehouse / Ohio General Assembly

Avoid

  • Partisan labels in voice copy unless legally required
  • "Activist" framing — use leader or organizer
  • Passive constructions
  • Naming peer advocacy organizations in comparison copy

Section 06

Messaging pillars

Power behind candidates
Putting money and political muscle behind pro-equality candidates who will act once in office.
Complement, don't duplicate
Advocacy organizations educate and lobby. Ohio Pride PAC builds on that work in a lane only a PAC can fill.
Nonpartisan and Ohio-focused
State-level, statehouse-first, anchored in Ohio's communities and Ohio's voters.
2026 urgency
Statewide offices, a special U.S. Senate election, statehouse races, and active anti-LGBTQ+ legislation make this a year that decides the next decade.

Section 07

Digital specs

Favicon system

A bold OH monogram on Navy Primary with a thin Progress Pride rainbow stripe. Standard sizes are shipped under /assets/favicon/ and referenced from the canonical <head> on every page.

32×32
16×16
180×180

Open Graph image

Default share image for social cards lives at /assets/social/og-image.png and is referenced from the <head> on every page via og:image and twitter:image.

Theme color

Mobile browser chrome uses Navy Primary via <meta name="theme-color" content="#0F2233">.

Tokens, helpers, and the consistency check

Every page consumes the canonical tokens at /css/brand-tokens.css and the shared chrome injected by /js/site-template.js. The npm run check:brand script walks every public HTML page in the repo and flags drift before a PR can land. See the internal docs at docs/brand-system.md for wiring details.
