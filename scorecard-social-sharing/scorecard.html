Scoring Methodology

Score Weights (v2.0-intersectional)

Every legislator is scored across four dimensions. Floor votes carry the most weight because a recorded vote is the clearest measure of how a legislator behaves when the moment of decision arrives. Sponsorship is weighted higher in v2 to reward members who drive the bills that protect our community, not just those who vote on them when someone else puts the question. Committee votes are tracked separately so members who stop bad bills in committee or advance good bills out of committee get the credit they deserve.

40%
Floor Votes
Recorded roll calls on chamber passage, concurrence, and veto overrides.
10%
Committee Votes
Votes to report a bill out of, or hold a bill in, committee.
30%
Sponsorship
Primary and co-sponsorship of pro- and anti-equality legislation.
20%
News and Statements
Public statements, press coverage, and floor speeches for or against LGBTQ+ Ohioans.

Event Weights

Inside each category, not every vote counts the same. Higher-stakes events are weighted up, procedural or amendment-only votes are weighted down.

  • Veto-override roll call: 1.25x
  • Concurrence or final passage: 1.00x
  • Chamber-of-origin passage: 1.00x
  • Committee report: 0.75x
  • Amendment-only vote: 0.50x

Intersectional Framing

LGBTQ+ equality cannot be separated from racial justice, reproductive freedom, voting rights, disability justice, and economic dignity. The same legislators who attack our community almost always attack the communities we belong to. Every legislator is evaluated across ten issue families that intersect with LGBTQ+ lives:

  • LGBTQ+ equality
  • Reproductive justice
  • Racial justice
  • Disability justice
  • Voting rights
  • Family, youth, and education
  • Immigrant justice
  • Criminal legal reform
  • Healthcare access
  • Expression and free speech

A cross-issue inconsistency flag is raised when a legislator's subscore dispersion across these ten families exceeds 35 points. That means the member is strong on one side of the equality map and weak on an intersecting one, and we surface that gap instead of letting it hide inside a composite score.

Source Hierarchy

When sources conflict, the higher-authority source wins. Provisional entries flagged in verificationStatus: "provisional" in the voting-records data file are reconciled against the chamber journal of record before being treated as canonical.

  1. Chamber journal of record (roll call)
  2. Legislative Service Commission bill analysis
  3. Committee minutes and clerk reports
  4. Primary-source news (hearing coverage, press events)
  5. Sponsor-authored statements and press releases
  6. Advocacy or opposition statements

Grade Scale

Raw scores are normalized to a 0-100 scale using max(0, min(100, 50 + raw * 5)), then the grade is applied.

Data Sources

  • Bill sponsorship and co-sponsorship records
  • Public statements, press conferences, and news coverage
  • Equality Ohio and TransOhio legislative tracking
  • ACLU Ohio legislative alerts
  • Adjacent civil-rights scorecards (NARAL Pro-Choice Ohio, NAACP Ohio Conference, ACLU Ohio, Ohio Voter Rights Coalition) for the intersectional component
